A Summary of Almost All Activism Ever

  1. Almost all political organisations throughout history organise around certain goals

  2. Members then supply the energy necessary to act collectively toward those shared goals

  3. Let's call this the classical strategic perspective

The Classical Strategic Perspective


revolution $=$ shared goals $\leftarrow$ energy

A Problem with Almost All Activism Ever


  1. Social complexity and diverse inequalities mean people have drastically different short- and medium-run goals

  2. Humans are being robbed of our most basic physical & emotional resources required to invest in collective action

  3. Therefore, a minor problem with the classical perspective is that it's increasingly and obviously useless

Activism Today


no revolution $=$ different goals $\leftarrow$ no energy

Yet activists remain captive to the classical perspective because we too are being robbed of the basic energies* which would allow us take more risks on each other and against status quo institutions

*Honesty, deep thought, care, transparency, vulnerability, attention, patience, trust, commitment, conviction, etc. Would anyone dare to claim they already possess a fully adequate store of such rare and precious weapons?
